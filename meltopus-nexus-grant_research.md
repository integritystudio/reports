## Executive summary

Chris Tong (**@meltopus**) is an Austin fire-performance artist and choreographer positioning to apply for a **City of Austin Nexus grant** — a **[$5,000 or $10,000](https://www.austintexas.gov/nexus)** award for emerging/established individual artists — using the **Museum of Human Achievement (MoHA)** as fiscal sponsor. The application is scored out of **100 points — Creative Work (35), Capacity (35), Public Connection (30)** — and panels reward work that "reflects the people, places, histories and cultures of Austin." The two decisive levers for a fire artist: (1) frame the project as **public community art, not commercial entertainment**, and (2) document a **1+ year Austin production history** (currently **undocumented** — the record lives in a private/gated Instagram; see §9) — both underpinned by Chris's community leadership: he is **Camp Lead of "Swolemates Safe Space,"** a queer-friendly strength-training camp at the regional Burning Man event **Burning Flipside** (organized by the 501(c)(3) Catalyst Collective), and hosts a **free, donation-only** strength event at his home **three times a week** year-round. Fire also raises a real operational bar: venue permits and liability insurance must be budgeted. Next window: **Round 2 opens September 2026**.

## 3. The Nexus grant — program mechanics

Nexus is the City of Austin ACME entry-tier cultural grant (HOT-funded), for "new and emerging local small organizations, individual artists, and small arts businesses." ([austintexas.gov/nexus](https://www.austintexas.gov/nexus); [createaustin.org/nexus](https://createaustin.org/nexus/))

- **Award:** [$5,000 or $10,000](https://www.austintexas.gov/nexus) based on score · **Term:** 12 months · **Cycles:** 2/year · **Fiscal sponsor:** allowed.

**Abbreviations:** ACME = Austin Arts, Culture, Music & Entertainment · HOT = Hotel Occupancy Tax · MSA = Metropolitan Statistical Area · ETJ = Extraterritorial Jurisdiction · MoHA = Museum of Human Achievement.

**Eligibility gates:**
- **1+ year creative-production history in Austin** before the deadline. ([austintexas.gov/nexus](https://www.austintexas.gov/nexus))
- Annual operating budget **≤ [$150,000](https://www.austintexas.gov/nexus)**. ([austintexas.gov/nexus](https://www.austintexas.gov/nexus))
- Events **open and advertised to Austin residents, visitors, and tourists**.
- Headquartered in the **Austin MSA**; production inside **Austin Council Districts or ETJ**.
- **501(c) nonprofits ineligible** — individuals apply directly or via a fiscal sponsor.

**Scoring rubric — 100 points:**

| Category | Points | What the panel assesses |
|---|---|---|
| Creative Work | 35 | Artistic merit, vision, work-sample strength |
| Capacity | 35 | Ability to deliver — experience, relationships, cultural knowledge, feasibility |
| Public Connection | 30 | Public benefit; reflects "the people, places, histories and cultures of Austin" |

Source: [austintexas.gov/nexus](https://www.austintexas.gov/nexus).

**Application steps (Submittable):** register → Intake Form → Eligibility Form → Application → work samples. One application per applicant per round. **If awarded:** complete a public activity, post to Visit Austin, and submit a final report with proof of funds spent + audience/participant data. Help: applicationhelp@thelongcenter.org; ACME workshops/office hours. ([createaustin.org/nexus](https://createaustin.org/nexus/))

## 4. Fiscal sponsorship via MoHA

- **No fiscal-sponsorship fee** — the artist keeps 100% of a Nexus award (Nexus otherwise permits a fee up to 10%). ([moha.wiki](https://moha.wiki/Fiscal_Sponsorship))
- Sponsors **individual artists** and informal/LLC orgs; **prioritizes Central Texas**.
- **Mission fit:** MoHA's grantee portfolio is **~61% LGBTQIA+** ([~$425K](https://moha.wiki/Fiscal_Sponsorship) to 140 first-time grantees) — aligned with Austin's queer, grassroots flow-arts community.
- Provides the 501(c)(3) umbrella for tax-deductible donations and grants without incorporating.

> **Critical Nexus + fiscal-sponsor mechanics:** the sponsor **may not** complete the Intake/Eligibility/Application — **Chris must initiate and submit personally**; MoHA is added as a **collaborator**. If awarded, the application/contract **transfers to MoHA at contracting**, and MoHA receives/disburses funds. Execute the MoHA Fiscal Sponsorship Agreement before the deadline. ([austintexas.gov/nexus](https://www.austintexas.gov/nexus); [createaustin.org/nexus](https://createaustin.org/nexus/))

## 5. Application strategy — scoring the 100-point rubric

| Category (pts) | How a fire choreographer wins the points |
|---|---|
| **Creative Work (35)** | Lead with choreography and artistic vision, not stunts. Submit tightly edited samples of *composed* fire pieces. Propose a specific new public work the grant funds. |
| **Capacity (35)** | Show delivery ability: 1+ yr Austin track record, named collaborators, realistic budget, and a plan for fire's hard parts (safety crew, permits, insurance). MoHA sponsorship signals admin capacity. |
| **Public Connection (30)** | Tie the work to Austin's people/places/histories/cultures: a free/low-barrier public performance, local flow-arts collaboration, accessible Austin-district venue. **Lead with the queer-community track record** — Swolemates Safe Space + the free donation-only home strength event show a lived commitment to accessible, non-commercial community space. |

**Highest-leverage moves:** (1) propose a **free, public, Austin-rooted premiere**; (2) name a **fire-safety + insurance plan** in the budget; (3) submit **choreographed** work samples, not gig-reel clips.
