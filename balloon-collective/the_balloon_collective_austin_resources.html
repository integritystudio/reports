1. Immediate Actions (Free & High Impact)

These cost nothing and directly address the visibility gaps identified in the research report. Each can be completed in under an hour.

Claim Google Business Profile
Critical Priority · Free · ~30 minutes

The research report identified that The Balloon Collective has no Google Business listing, making it invisible to local "balloon decor Austin" searches. Competitors like Balloon People TX (219 photos) and Balloon Boutique (22 reviews) dominate local search results.

  • Go to business.google.com and sign in with a Google account
  • Enter "The Balloon Collective" and follow the prompts to create a new listing
  • Select category: Balloon Artist or Party & Event Planning Service
  • Add South Austin as service area (hide exact address if home-based)
  • Upload 10–20 high-quality portfolio photos immediately
  • Verify via mail, phone, or video — typically takes 3–14 days
Create Google Business Profile →
Create Yelp Business Page
Critical Priority · Free · ~20 minutes

No Yelp page exists. In the Austin balloon decor market, Yelp is a primary discovery channel. Balloon Boutique has 22 reviews and ATX Bubbles has 16 reviews on Yelp.

  • Visit business.yelp.com and click "Manage my free listing"
  • Add business details, photos, service descriptions, and hours
  • Respond to any reviews that appear after claiming
Claim Yelp Business →

2. Grants & Funding Programs

Austin and Texas offer multiple grant and funding programs relevant to creative small businesses. The Balloon Collective's work in immersive art installations (Hopscotch, Austin Pride Parade) may qualify for arts-specific funding.

Time-Sensitive: The City of Austin AACME department distributes over $21M in arts grants from the Hotel Occupancy Tax. The Nexus Grant Round 1 opens March 2026 ($5K–$10K). Virtual office hours are available Tuesdays 10am–12pm through March 4. The Texas Commission on the Arts FY27 deadline is March 2–16, 2026.

3. Business Certifications

Certifications open doors to government contracts, corporate supplier diversity programs, and preferential vendor status. The Balloon Collective's LLC structure and Austin location make it eligible for multiple programs.

Prerequisites: Register as a vendor with the City of Austin via Austin Finance Online before beginning any City certification application.
City of Austin Certifications 2 certifications
MBE Certification (Minority Business Enterprise)
High Priority · Free · Renews every 4 years

A City of Austin certification for small businesses that are at least 51% owned, managed, and controlled by one or more economically disadvantaged minorities. Nate Culpepper's ownership may qualify.

  • Requirements: 51% minority ownership, meet SBA small business size standards, Austin-area operations
  • Benefits: Access to city contracts with MBE participation goals, vendor matchmaking events, SMBR networking
  • Process: Complete online application on the CCS portal after vendor registration
  • Renewal: Every 4 years via online recertification
Apply for MBE Certification →
WBE Certification (Women's Business Enterprise)
High Priority · Free · Renews every 4 years

For businesses that are at least 51% owned, managed, and controlled by one or more economically disadvantaged women. If Stacy Wheeler holds qualifying ownership, this certification is directly applicable.

  • Requirements: 51% woman ownership, meet SBA size standards, Austin-area operations
  • Benefits: Same as MBE — city contract access, networking events, SMBR support
  • Contact: (512) 974-7645 or SMBRCertification@austintexas.gov
Apply for WBE Certification →
State & National Certifications 3 certifications
Texas HUB Certification (Historically Underutilized Business)
State · Free · Ongoing

Texas Comptroller program that certifies minority-owned, women-owned, and service-disabled veteran-owned businesses for state contracting preference. State agencies have HUB participation goals.

  • Requirements: Texas-based, for-profit, 51% owned by qualifying individual(s)
  • Benefits: Access to $40B+ in annual Texas state purchasing, HUB directory listing, state agency matchmaking
  • Application: Online through the TX Comptroller's office
TX HUB Certification →
WBENC National Certification (Women's Business Enterprise)
National · $350–$1,500 fee · 15–90 days

The gold standard for women-owned business certification, recognized by 1,000+ corporations for supplier diversity programs. The regional affiliate is the Women's Business Enterprise Alliance (WBEA) based in Texas.

  • Requirements: 51% woman ownership, U.S. citizenship, independent business
  • Benefits: Corporate supplier diversity programs (Dell, Apple, Google, Meta — all Austin employers), national networking events
  • Regional contact: WBEA — Women's Business Enterprise Alliance
WBENC.org →
SBA 8(a) Business Development Program
Federal · Free · 9-year program

Federal program for small, disadvantaged businesses providing access to sole-source federal contracts, mentorship, and business development support. Significant competitive advantage for government event contracts.

  • Requirements: Unconditionally owned (51%+) by socially/economically disadvantaged individual(s), under $750K net worth, under SBA size standards
  • Benefits: Sole-source federal contracts up to $4.5M, mentorship, joint ventures with large contractors
SBA 8(a) Program →
