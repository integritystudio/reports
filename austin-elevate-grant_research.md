# Recipients of Austin's Elevate Grant — Overview

**Comprehensive Research Report** · Austin Arts, Culture, Music & Entertainment (ACME) · Prepared July 2026
Primary source: [austintexas.gov/arts-culture/elevate](https://www.austintexas.gov/arts-culture/elevate)

## Executive summary

Elevate is the largest of the City of Austin's Hotel Occupancy Tax (HOT)–funded cultural-arts grants, administered by the Arts, Culture, Music & Entertainment (ACME) office. In its **FY26 cycle (awarded March 2026), Elevate funded 288–289 organizations, groups, and individual artists with [$12.84–$12.86 million](https://www.austintexas.gov/communications/news/city-austin-announces-2026-cultural-funding-awards)**, in awards from **[$4,000 to $80,000](https://www.austintexas.gov/communications/news/city-austin-announces-2026-cultural-funding-awards)**. Recipients span three applicant types — **501(c)(3) arts nonprofits, arts groups/creative businesses, and individual artists** — across every Austin council district plus the ETJ. Demand far outstrips supply: across ACME's four programs, **1,606 applicants requested [$67M+](https://www.austintexas.gov/communications/news/city-austin-announces-2026-cultural-funding-awards) against [$24M](https://www.austintexas.gov/communications/news/city-austin-announces-2026-cultural-funding-awards) awarded**. The recipient pool is notably inclusive — program-wide, **26.6% of awardees identify as LGBTQ+**, and Black and Asian applicants were funded above their share of the applicant pool. For a movement-arts organization, Elevate's flexible eligibility (fiscal sponsors permitted) and explicit support for "dance and movement-based work" make it a realistic core funding channel.

> **Scope & data note:** This is an *overview of the Elevate recipient population* — who qualifies, how many are funded, at what amounts, and their disciplines/demographics — not a line-by-line roster. The City publishes full named awardee lists as downloadable files (linked below). The **discipline and demographic percentages are reported ACME-wide across all four FY26 programs (731 awardees), not Elevate alone** — the City does not publish an Elevate-only demographic split. Dollar figures and tiers are time-sensitive.

## 1. Program overview

Elevate "invests in cultural organizations, groups, and individual artists that create vibrant, publicly accessible artistic experiences," funding creative production, public programming, and operational/administrative expenses. It is one of four HOT-funded ACME programs, alongside the **Austin Live Music Fund**, **Creative Space Assistance Program (CSAP)**, and **Heritage Preservation Grant**. Eligible activities explicitly include "dance and movement-based work." ([austintexas.gov/arts-culture/elevate](https://www.austintexas.gov/arts-culture/elevate); [KUT](https://www.kut.org/life-arts/2026-07-08/austin-tx-arts-music-grants-applications-open-acme))

**Abbreviations:** ACME = Austin Arts, Culture, Music & Entertainment · HOT = Hotel Occupancy Tax · ETJ = Extraterritorial Jurisdiction · MSA = Metropolitan Statistical Area · CSAP = Creative Space Assistance Program · FY = Fiscal Year (Oct 1–Sep 30).

## 2. Who receives Elevate — recipient profile

**Three eligible applicant types:**
- **501(c)(3) arts nonprofits** — no annual-budget cap.
- **State-registered arts nonprofits & arts groups / creative businesses** — up to [$500K](https://www.austintexas.gov/arts-culture/elevate) annual budget.
- **Individual artists** — up to [$500K](https://www.austintexas.gov/arts-culture/elevate); **may apply through a fiscal sponsor** if lacking their own 501(c)(3).

Thresholds: **2+ years of Austin production history**; publicly advertised events; **headquartered in the Austin MSA with >50% of production inside the Austin 10-1 districts or ETJ**; max **one Cultural Arts Fund grant** (Thrive/Elevate/Nexus/Live Music) per fiscal year. ([austintexas.gov/arts-culture/elevate](https://www.austintexas.gov/arts-culture/elevate))

## 4. Discipline & demographic snapshot

**Important:** figures below are **ACME-wide across all four FY26 programs (731 awardees)**, not Elevate-only. Music is inflated by the separate Live Music Fund (399 grantees). ([austintexas.gov/acme/grants-funding](https://www.austintexas.gov/acme/grants-funding))

**Discipline (FY26, all ACME):** Music 60.6% (422) · Multidisciplinary 7.9% (55) · Theater/Musical Theatre/Opera 4.0% (28) · Film/Video/TV 3.7% (26) · Cultural Heritage 3.6% (25) · Arts Education 3.4% (24) · **Dance 3.3% (23)** · Visual Arts 2.4% (17) · Literary 1.4% (10).

**Race/ethnicity (lead applicants):** White 46.3% (322) · Hispanic 18.5% (129) · Black 12.4% (86) · Multiracial 6.6% (46) · Asian 6.2% (43) · Other 9.9%.

**Community:** LGBTQ+ 26.6% (185) · Disability 11.1% (77) · 501(c)(3) nonprofits 173.

**Equity signal:** Black (12.5% of applicants) and Asian (6.2%) applicants were funded at rates above their applicant share; LGBTQIA+ creatives saw "consistently strong acceptance rates." ([City 2026 grants launch](https://www.austintexas.gov/communications/news/city-austin-launches-23-million-grants-local-creative-community))

## 5. Competitiveness & demand

Elevate is heavily oversubscribed. FY26: **1,606–1,607 applications requesting [$67M+](https://www.austintexas.gov/communications/news/city-austin-announces-2026-cultural-funding-awards)** across four programs; **731 grants / [$24M+](https://www.austintexas.gov/communications/news/city-austin-announces-2026-cultural-funding-awards)** awarded — roughly **36¢ per dollar requested**. Elevate itself funded **288** awards. FY27 opened with **[$11M](https://www.austintexas.gov/arts-culture/elevate) available for Elevate**. ([City 2026 awards release](https://www.austintexas.gov/communications/news/city-austin-announces-2026-cultural-funding-awards); [ACME funding](https://www.austintexas.gov/acme/grants-funding))

## 6. Geographic distribution

FY26 awards reached **all 10 council districts plus ETJ and MSA**. **District 9** led with **125 awardees / [$5,466,823](https://www.austintexas.gov/acme/grants-funding)**. The >50%-production-in-Austin/ETJ rule concentrates recipients among city-rooted organizations. ([austintexas.gov/acme/grants-funding](https://www.austintexas.gov/acme/grants-funding))

## 8. Requirements & mechanics for recipients

- **Liability insurance required of all awardees.** ([createaustin.org/elevate](https://createaustin.org/elevate/))
- **Payment in three tranches:** 50% on paperwork; 40% after a Miles Partnership class or Visit Austin post; 10% after final reporting.
- **Terms:** one- or two-year agreements; funding begins January.
- **FY27 cycle:** applications **July 7 – August 18, 2026 (6:59 p.m.)** via Submittable; [$11M](https://www.austintexas.gov/arts-culture/elevate) available.
- **Support:** workshops, office hours, staff meetings, instructional videos (English & Spanish).

> "These HOT-funded grants are not just awards, they are a reinvestment of visitor dollars back into the artists, musicians, cultural organizations and heritage stewards who make Austin a sought-out destination." — Angela Means, Director, Austin ACME ([KUT](https://www.kut.org/life-arts/2026-07-08/austin-tx-arts-music-grants-applications-open-acme))

## 9. Implications for a movement-arts applicant

See companion report: [Founding a 501(c)(3) Movement Arts Nonprofit in Austin](movement-arts-nonprofit-austin.html).

- **No 501(c)(3) needed to start** — individuals/groups may apply through a fiscal sponsor (ACA, MoHA), so a fiscally sponsored movement-arts project can access Elevate before incorporating.
- **Dance/movement-based work is explicitly eligible**, and Dance is a small, lower-competition share of the pool (3.3%, 23 awardees).
- **The pool is strongly LGBTQ+** (26.6%), aligning with an affirming mission.
- **Plan for:** 2+ years documented Austin production history, liability insurance (already essential for fire/aerial), >50% production in Austin/ETJ.
- **Sequence:** Nexus ([$5K–$10K](https://www.austintexas.gov/arts-culture/funding-programs)) → Elevate → Thrive (after 5 yrs, [$60K+](https://www.austintexas.gov/thrive) budget).

## 11. Conclusions & recommendations

- Elevate funds a broad, inclusive cross-section (~288 recipients/yr) at [$4,000–$80,000](https://www.austintexas.gov/communications/news/city-austin-announces-2026-cultural-funding-awards).
- Music dominates ACME's overall numbers due to the separate Live Music Fund; within the arts grants proper, disciplines are more balanced and dance/movement is a defined, lower-competition niche.
- The pool is diverse and LGBTQ+-heavy by design.
- It is very competitive (~36¢ awarded per requested dollar) — a specific, Austin-rooted proposal with documented history matters.

**Next research steps:** download the FY26 Elevate list to profile named dance/movement recipients and typical award sizes; compare 2023–24 nonprofit vs. individual success; request an Elevate-only demographic split from ACME (culturalarts@austintexas.gov).
