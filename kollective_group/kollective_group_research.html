Executive Summary

Kollective Group is a boutique executive search and recruitment firm headquartered in San Antonio, TX (Travis Park Plaza, 711 Navarro St, 78205), specializing in Sales, Marketing, and Technology placements. The firm offers three service models — Recruitment Process Outsourcing (RPO), retained search, and contingency search — targeting high-growth retail, e-commerce, and technology companies. Kollective Group is a small operation (2–11 employees per third-party sources; 11–50 per LinkedIn) with one confirmed team member: Nicki (Rumanek) Geary, a recruitment specialist based in Austin, TX. The firm claims over a decade of recruitment experience but discloses no founding year, named founders, client logos, or case studies. No third-party reviews, BBB listing, or Google Business Profile exists. The global executive search market is valued at $63.99 billion (2026) growing at 10.11% CAGR, while the RPO market is projected to reach $22.9 billion by 2030 at 15.4% CAGR — both segments show strong tailwinds for the firm’s business model.

11. Conclusions and Recommendations

Key Findings

  1. Minimal public footprint: Kollective Group has zero third-party reviews, no named leadership, no disclosed clients, and no published case studies — making independent quality assessment impossible.
  2. Favorable market positioning: The firm’s Sales/Marketing/Technology specialization aligns with the highest-growth recruiting verticals. RPO at 15–17% CAGR and executive search at 10% CAGR provide strong sector tailwinds.
  3. Competitive disadvantage on trust signals: Every identified competitor has at least one of: named founders, client logos, platform reviews, or industry awards. Kollective Group has none.
  4. Active but opaque operations: LinkedIn job postings confirm active recruiting in NYC and Chicago markets, yet the company’s own jobs board is empty and no placement metrics are disclosed.
  5. Strong regional opportunity: Austin’s 203,000 tech workers, $4.5B in VC, and 79,580 business establishments create substantial recruiting demand.
