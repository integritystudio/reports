Eligibility Caveats

What you keep as a veteran: Lifetime NFCU and USAA membership eligibility. This is the durable, high-value benefit — both institutions offer rates and products unavailable to the general public.

Strategy

The Arbitrage Play

With low net worth and a $0 annual fee card, the highest-ROI move is purchase float arbitrage.

Purchase Float → HYSA → Pay In Full

  1. Apply for Wells Fargo Reflect (21 months, 0% purchases, $0 annual fee)
  2. Route all monthly essentials through the card (groceries, utilities, subscriptions)
  3. Keep equivalent cash in a HYSA earning 4.0–5.0% APY
  4. Pay the statement minimum monthly; pay full balance in month 20
  5. Set a calendar reminder for month 19 — do not miss the payoff deadline
$1,000/month floated × 21 months = $21,000 average float • At 4.5% APY ≈ $900–$1,000 earned • Card cost: $0

HYSA picks (May 19, 2026): Varo 5.00% APY • SoFi 4.50% • CIT Bank 4.10%. Rates are trending down — verify before opening.

Risk

Pitfalls

  • Deferred interest: None of the listed cards use deferred interest (retail/store cards do). Confirm the Schumer Box anyway before applying. With deferred interest, missing the promo end triggers retroactive interest on the original balance.
  • Balance transfer fee math: A 3% BT fee on an 18-month hold breaks even against ~2% APR. Any card charging above ~2% APR makes a BT worthwhile even with a fee. The NFCU Platinum no-fee structure is genuinely better math for ≤12-month payoffs.
  • Penalty APR: Most major issuers (Chase, Wells Fargo, BoA) can trigger penalty APR (up to 29.99%) after one late payment. Set autopay to at least the minimum. Citi Simplicity is the only card listed with no penalty APR.
  • Credit utilization during float: Carrying a large balance temporarily raises reported utilization, which may lower your 805 score. This matters only if you plan to apply for a mortgage or auto loan during the float window.
  • USAA purchase promo: USAA purchase 0% intro data conflicts across sources. Treat as unconfirmed. Verify at usaa.com directly.

Backup — BT Use Case

NFCU Platinum

If the goal is consolidating existing high-APR debt, the no-BT-fee structure saves $200+ per $10K vs. any fee-based 0% card. Ongoing 10.24% floor is the best backstop available.

  1. Apply for Wells Fargo Reflect at wellsfargo.com — 805 FICO, expect approval and a high limit.
  2. Open or verify a HYSA (Varo, SoFi, or CIT Bank) before the first Reflect statement closes.
  3. If carrying existing high-interest debt: apply for NFCU Platinum at navyfederal.org (veteran eligibility is permanent; no BT fee saves real money).
  4. Set autopay on both cards to at least the minimum the day they are opened.
  5. Add a calendar reminder 20 months from Reflect approval date to pay the full balance.
  6. Do not apply for additional credit within 6 months if any major financing (mortgage, auto) is planned.
