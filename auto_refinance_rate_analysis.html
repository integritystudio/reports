1. Executive Summary

For a borrower with an 800+ FICO score refinancing $34,180 over 72 months in March 2026, the best available rates range from 3.89% to 5.39% APR depending on the lender and vehicle age. With the Federal Reserve holding the federal funds rate at 3.50–3.75%, auto loan rates remain elevated compared to pre-2022 levels but have come down from 2024 peaks following three rate cuts in late 2025. The difference between the best rate (Navy Federal at 3.89% for new vehicles) and the industry average for super-prime borrowers at 72 months (~5.60%) translates to approximately $1,943 in total interest savings over the life of the loan. Credit unions consistently offer the most competitive refinance rates, particularly for borrowers in the Austin metro area.

8. Recommendations

Action Plan

  1. Apply at 2–3 credit unions first. UFCU (5.64%–5.89%) and Austin Telco (5.24%) are locally accessible. If eligible, Navy Federal (4.59%–5.39%) offers the best published rates.
  2. Get pre-qualified at Capital One as a bank benchmark (soft pull, no credit impact). Their 5.33% new / 6.11% used rates serve as a ceiling to beat.
  3. Rate-shop within a 14-day window. Multiple hard inquiries for the same loan type within 14–45 days (depending on scoring model) count as a single inquiry on your credit report.
  4. Negotiate with your current lender. Present competing offers to your existing loan servicer—they may match or beat to retain the loan.
  5. Consider waiting if not urgent. If the Fed cuts 1–2 more times in 2026, rates could improve by another 25–50 bps. However, the savings from refinancing now at ~5% vs. waiting for ~4.5% on $34,180 over 72 months is only ~$568 total—not worth delaying if you're currently paying 6%+.

Break-Even Analysis

If your current rate is 6.5% or higher, refinancing to 5.0% saves ~$24/month ($1,735 total interest savings). With most lenders charging zero refinance fees, the break-even is immediate. Even at a modest improvement from 6.0% to 5.0%, you save ~$16/month ($1,152 total).

72-Month Term Considerations

Longer terms cost more. A 72-month term on $34,180 at 5.0% costs $5,454 in total interest. The same loan at 60 months and 5.0% costs $4,521—saving $932 but increasing the monthly payment by $95 ($645 vs. $550). Evaluate whether the lower monthly payment justifies the additional interest cost for your budget.
