## Example Campaigns

### 1. Energy Drink (Young Adults)

**Brief**: New energy drink, target 18-30, goal: awareness + trial

**Audience Profile**
- Gamers, college students, shift workers, fitness enthusiasts
- High digital/social media consumption
- Values: energy, performance, authenticity, affordability
- Pain points: Afternoon crash, expensive premium drinks, artificial ingredients

**Key Messages**
- "Real Energy. Real Price."
- Authentic, no crash, affordable performance fuel
- Tone: Direct, energetic, authentic (no corporate-speak)

**Slogans** (tested for Gen Z appeal)
1. "Crash-Free Energy" — emphasizes unique benefit
2. "Performance, Honestly Priced" — value positioning
3. "Your Energy Shouldn't Cost Everything" — affordability angle

**Media Strategy**
- **Primary**: TikTok (15-20% of budget), Twitch streaming partnerships (10%), YouTube (15%)
- **Secondary**: Instagram Reels (10%), Reddit gaming communities (5%)
- **Tertiary**: College campus activations (8%), convenience store displays (10%)
- **Digital**: Search ads for "best energy drink" keywords (15%)
- **Traditional**: Minimal TV/radio (5%), targeted local radio in gaming markets

**Activation & Partnerships**
- Esports team sponsorships (partnered with gaming influencers)
- College ambassador program (50 campus reps)
- Twitch streamer sampling campaign
- In-game advertising (Fortnite, League of Legends)
- College gym/fitness center partnerships
- Convenience store promotional events
- Social media challenges (#RealEnergyChallenge)

**Budget Allocation** ($200K campaign)
- Social media creative & paid: 35%
- Influencer partnerships: 20%
- Campus/ground activation: 15%
- Search & digital: 15%
- Traditional media: 10%
- Contingency: 5%

**KPIs**
- TikTok: 5M+ impressions, 2%+ engagement rate
- Trial acquisition: 50K units distributed
- Brand awareness (target demo): 30%+ awareness lift
- CPA: <$2 per trial

**Timeline**
- Week 1-2: Influencer onboarding, creative production
- Week 3-4: Soft launch with influencers, test campaigns
- Week 5-12: Full media spend rollout
- Week 13+: Optimization based on performance data

### 2. Sustainable Fashion Brand (Millennials)

**Brief**: Eco-conscious fashion, target millennials 25-40, goal: brand awareness + premium positioning

**Audience Profile**
- College-educated, urban, $50K+ income
- Values: sustainability, ethics, quality over quantity
- Media: Instagram, sustainability blogs, podcasts, independent media
- Purchase driver: Environmental/social impact alignment

**Key Messages**
- Sustainable doesn't mean compromise on style
- Transparency in supply chain
- Building a conscious movement, not just selling clothes

**Slogans**
1. "Wear Your Values" — values alignment
2. "Fashion with a Conscience" — ethics positioning
3. "Beautiful + Responsible" — quality + sustainability

**Media Strategy**
- **Primary**: Instagram (20%), Sustainable fashion blogs (15%), TikTok (15%)
- **Secondary**: LinkedIn (10%), Podcast sponsorships (10%)
- **Tertiary**: Local zero-waste retail partnerships (10%), sustainability magazines (5%)
- **Digital**: Google Ads for "sustainable fashion" (10%)
- **Experiential**: Pop-up clothing swaps, trunk shows in eco-hubs (5%)

**Activation & Partnerships**
- Micro-influencer partnerships (100+ sustainability-focused creators)
- Documentary collaboration highlighting supply chain
- Zero-waste retail takeovers in major cities
- Clothing swap/rental events in partner cities
- Sustainability newsletter features
- Corporate gifting partnerships with ESG-focused companies

**Budget Allocation** ($150K campaign)
- Influencer marketing: 35%
- Digital/paid social: 25%
- Content creation: 15%
- Experiential events: 15%
- PR & partnerships: 10%

**KPIs**
- Instagram followers growth: 50K+ new followers
- Engagement rate: 4-6% (above fashion industry average)
- Website traffic from campaigns: 100K+ sessions
- Conversion rate: 3-5%
- PR mentions in sustainability publications: 20+

### 3. Premium Coffee Subscription (Remote Workers)

**Brief**: Coffee subscription service, target remote professionals 25-50, goal: trial + retention

**Audience Profile**
- High income, productivity-focused, home office invested
- Appreciate premium quality and convenience
- Early adopters of SaaS, subscription models
- Media: LinkedIn, podcasts, email, productivity blogs

**Key Messages**
- Elevate your home office experience
- Premium quality, delivered to your door, consistent every day
- Join a community of coffee enthusiasts who work from home

**Slogans**
1. "Your Office Upgrade" — workspace elevation
2. "Better Coffee, Every Morning" — quality consistency
3. "Remote Work Elevated" — lifestyle positioning

**Media Strategy**
- **Primary**: LinkedIn (25%), Productivity podcasts (20%)
- **Secondary**: Email (20%), Workspace design blogs (10%)
- **Tertiary**: Slack integrations (8%), Coworking spaces (7%)
- **Digital**: Google Ads for "premium coffee" + "home office" (10%)

**Activation & Partnerships**
- Free trial partnerships with coworking spaces and virtual offices
- Webinar series: "Optimizing Your Remote Work Setup"
- Podcast sponsorships (How I Work, The Indicator, similar shows)
- LinkedIn thought leadership content
- Slack bot for reorder reminders
- Corporate subscription discounts for distributed teams
- Community Slack group for subscribers

**Budget Allocation** ($100K campaign)
- Podcast sponsorships: 30%
- Digital/email: 25%
- LinkedIn advertising: 20%
- Partnerships & events: 15%
- Content creation: 10%

**KPIs**
- Trial signups: 5,000+
- Trial-to-paid conversion: 25%+
- Customer acquisition cost: <$30
- Lifetime value: $200+ (annual retention goal)
- Podcast listener conversion: 2-3%

## Campaign Customization Prompts

For best results, specify:
1. **Product/Service**: What are you selling?
2. **Target Audience**: Who (demographics, psychographics)?
3. **Goals**: What do you want to achieve (awareness, trial, sales, loyalty)?
4. **Budget**: How much can you spend? (helps with channel prioritization)
5. **Timeline**: When do you need to launch?
6. **Competitive Context**: Who are your main competitors?
7. **Unique Angle**: What's your differentiator or unique story?
