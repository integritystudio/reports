Key Findings Summary

Consumer-Focused Highlights

  • Federal EV credit expired: September 30, 2025—replaced by loan interest deduction (up to $10k/year for qualified buyers)
  • Manufacturer incentives strong: 49 zero-percent APR offers nationally; cash rebates up to $10,000
  • Hybrid market surging: +9.1% YoY growth in February 2026; 33% of shoppers targeting hybrids
  • Lease deals competitive: Best lease rates under $200/month (Toyota Corolla @ $179/mo)
  • Average vehicle price: $49,170 (Feb 2026); tariff pressures expected to add ~3% inflation by mid-2026

Austin Market Highlights

  • Addressable market: ~18,250–22,750 vehicles annually (5–7% penetration of 520k owner-occupied homes)
  • Segment dominance: 59% SUV/crossover preference (highest nationally)
  • Demographics: Median income $89,950 (+20.6% vs. US), median age 34.5 (younger), population growth 2.0% annually
  • Powertrain mix: 89.8% ICE+Hybrid; hybrids growing; EV share projected to soften to ~6% by year-end
  • Growth driver: Millennials entering replacement cycles; tech-industry employment supporting premium purchases
