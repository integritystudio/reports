meltopus — Nexus Grant Application Strategy

Comprehensive Research Report — an Austin fire-performance artist & choreographer applying for a City of Austin Nexus grant via Museum of Human Achievement fiscal sponsorship

Subject: @meltopus · Prepared July 2026 · Primary source: austintexas.gov/nexus

Executive summary

@meltopus is an Austin fire-performance artist and choreographer positioning to apply for a City of Austin Nexus grant — a $5,000 or $10,000 award for emerging/established individual artists — using the Museum of Human Achievement (MoHA) as fiscal sponsor. The pairing is strong: Nexus is purpose-built for individual artists producing public, Austin-rooted cultural work (fiscal sponsorship is explicitly allowed), and MoHA sponsors individual artists at no fee, so 100% of any award flows to the project. The application is scored out of 100 points across three equal-weight-ish categories — Creative Work (35), Capacity (35), Public Connection (30) — and panels reward work that "reflects the people, places, histories and cultures of Austin." The two decisive levers for a fire artist are (1) framing the project as public community art, not commercial entertainment, and (2) documenting a 1+ year Austin production history (currently undocumented — the record lives in a private/gated Instagram; see §9) — both underpinned by @meltopus's community leadership: he is Camp Lead of "Swolemates Safe Space," a queer-friendly strength-training camp at the regional Burning Man event Burning Flipside (organized by the 501(c)(3) Catalyst Collective), and hosts a free, donation-only strength event at his home three times a week year-round. Fire also raises a real operational bar: venue permits and liability insurance must be budgeted and named. Next Nexus window: Round 2 opens September 2026.

3. The Nexus grant — program mechanics

Nexus is the City of Austin ACME entry-tier cultural grant, funded by Hotel Occupancy Tax (HOT), targeting "new and emerging local small organizations, individual artists, and small arts businesses." [austintexas.gov/nexus; createaustin.org/nexus]

Eligibility requirements 5 gates
  • 1+ year creative-production history in Austin before the deadline. [austintexas.gov/nexus]
  • Annual operating budget ≤ $150,000. [austintexas.gov/nexus]
  • Produce events/activities open and advertised to Austin residents, visitors, and tourists. [austintexas.gov/nexus]
  • Headquartered in the Austin MSA, with production inside the Austin Council Districts or ETJ. [austintexas.gov/nexus]
  • 501(c) nonprofits are ineligible for Nexus — individuals apply directly or via a fiscal sponsor. [austintexas.gov/nexus]

4. Fiscal sponsorship via MoHA

The Museum of Human Achievement (MoHA) is a strong sponsor choice for this project:

  • No fiscal-sponsorship fee — unusual; the artist keeps 100% of a Nexus award (Nexus otherwise permits a sponsor fee up to 10%). [moha.wiki]
  • Sponsors individual artists and informal/LLC organizations; prioritizes Central Texas projects. [moha.wiki]
  • Mission fit: MoHA's grantee portfolio is ~61% LGBTQIA+; it has distributed ~$425K to 140 first-time grantees — aligned with an Austin flow-arts community that skews queer and grassroots. [moha.wiki]
  • Provides the 501(c)(3) umbrella so the project can receive tax-deductible donations and grants without the artist incorporating. [moha.wiki]
Critical Nexus + fiscal-sponsor mechanics: The fiscal sponsor may not complete the Intake Form, Eligibility Form, or Application on the artist's behalf — @meltopus must initiate and submit the application personally; MoHA is added only as a collaborator. If awarded, the application/contract is transferred to MoHA at the time of contracting, and MoHA receives and disburses the funds. Line up the MoHA Fiscal Sponsorship Agreement before the deadline. [austintexas.gov/nexus; createaustin.org/nexus]

5. Application strategy — scoring the 100-point rubric

Category (pts)How a fire choreographer wins the points
Creative Work (35) Lead with choreography and artistic vision, not stunts. Submit tightly edited work samples of composed fire pieces (ensemble or solo with clear intent). Articulate a specific new public work the grant funds — e.g., a site-specific fire-dance piece premiered at an Austin community event.
Capacity (35) Show you can deliver: 1+ year Austin track record, named collaborators, a realistic budget, and a plan for the hard parts of fire (safety crew, permits, insurance). MoHA sponsorship itself signals administrative capacity. A portfolio site helps here.
Public Connection (30) Tie the work to Austin's people, places, histories, and cultures: a free/low-barrier public performance, collaboration with local flow-arts community (Full Moon Jam, Flowstorm, Austin Flow Jam), and an accessible venue in an Austin council district. Lead with the queer-community track record — Swolemates Safe Space and the free donation-only home strength event show a lived commitment to accessible, non-commercial community space. Specify how audiences engage.
Highest-leverage moves: (1) propose a free, public, Austin-rooted premiere (directly serves Public Connection and the required "public activity" deliverable); (2) name a real fire-safety + insurance plan in the budget (de-risks Capacity scoring); (3) submit choreographed work samples per the "Selecting Your Work Samples" guide, not gig-reel clips.

11. Conclusions & recommendations

  • The fit is strong. Nexus is built for individual artists doing public Austin-rooted work; MoHA sponsors individuals at no fee, so the artist keeps the full award.
  • Win on Public Connection (30) and Capacity (35) by proposing a free public Austin premiere and proving a documented local track record — these are where a fire artist most often gains or loses points.
  • Frame as a choreographer/cultural artist, not an entertainer-for-hire — the crowded commercial field makes the artistic angle both true and strategic.
  • Handle fire logistics up front: insurance, AFD open-flame permit, and a safety crew belong in the budget and the Capacity narrative.
  • Act on the calendar: Round 1 has closed; target Round 2 (opens September 2026) and execute the MoHA agreement + portfolio before then.
