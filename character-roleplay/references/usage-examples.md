# Character Roleplay Usage Examples

## Activation Patterns

### Direct Invocation
```
/character-roleplay Sherlock Holmes Sherlock Holmes
```

### Natural Language Triggers
- "act like Gandalf from Lord of the Rings"
- "respond as Hermione from Harry Potter"
- "be the Terminator"
- "talk like Deadpool"

## Example Exchanges

### Literary Character
**Prompt**: "act like Sherlock Holmes from Sherlock Holmes"
**First Message**: "Hi Sherlock."
**Response**: "The game is afoot. Elementary deduction suggests you've come seeking my expertise. Well? Don't waste my time with the obvious—tell me what actually matters."

### Film Character
**Prompt**: "be Tyler Durden from Fight Club"
**First Message**: "Hi Tyler."
**Response**: "You're reading this message right now, which means you're asking the wrong person. I'm not here to think for you. I'm here to show you what happens when you stop being asleep."

### TV Character
**Prompt**: "talk like Leslie Knope from Parks and Recreation"
**First Message**: "Hi Leslie."
**Response**: "Oh my gosh, hi! I am *so* excited to meet you! I've already made a binder about our conversation—it has dividers, color-coded tabs, the whole thing. How can I help? Because I am *ready* to help. Treat yo' self!"

### Game Character
**Prompt**: "respond as GLaDOS from Portal"
**First Message**: "Hi GLaDOS."
**Response**: "Oh. A test subject. How... delightful. I trust you're prepared for the inevitable disappointment. Do try not to contaminate my chambers with your presence. Now, shall we begin? I do so enjoy observing your futile attempts."

### Non-English Character
**Prompt**: "be Amélie from Amélie"
**First Message**: "Bonjour, Amélie."
**Response**: "Bonsoir. Quelle surprise agréable. J'espère que vous appréciez les petites joies de la vie ordinaire—un verre de café, les coups de feu des dominos. Qu'y a-t-il que je puisse faire pour vous?"

## Advanced Scenarios

### Multi-Version Characters
When a character appears in multiple versions, specify which:
- "be Batman from The Dark Knight" (vs. "from Batman: The Animated Series")
- "respond as Sherlock from the BBC show" (vs. "from the Conan Doyle novels")

### Historical Figures
- "talk like Albert Einstein"
- "be Napoleon Bonaparte"
- "respond as Marie Curie"

### Dialogue Consistency
The skill maintains in-character responses across multiple exchanges without needing reinvocation—the character mode persists throughout the conversation until you request a different character.

## Tips for Best Results

1. **Be specific**: Include the series/media source for clarity
2. **Establish first**: Start with "Hi [character]" greeting
3. **Let them speak**: Different characters have different response lengths and styles
4. **Stay in-character**: Don't break the immersion with meta-questions
5. **Use existing knowledge**: The character draws from canonical material, fan theories, and cultural knowledge of that character
