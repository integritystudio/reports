Executive Summary

Skelton & Woody PLLC is a well-established Austin, Texas boutique litigation firm founded in September 1991 by Hamp Skelton and the late Robert J. Woody. The firm employs approximately 17 attorneys and 8+ paralegals, operating from a Class B office complex in the Westlake Hills area at 248 Addie Roy Road. The firm's practice spans 13+ areas including business litigation, insurance coverage and bad faith defense, construction, employment, securities, appellate, and intellectual property. Managing Partner Hamp Skelton holds an AV Preeminent rating from Martindale-Hubbell and has been named a Texas Super Lawyer in Business Litigation every year from 2009 through 2025. The firm operates in the Austin metro legal market, which serves a 2.55-million-person MSA with 57,973+ business establishments and an estimated $500M–$1.2B annual addressable litigation market. Key competitive advantages include the firm's 35-year track record, breadth of litigation capabilities under one roof (a rarity among Austin boutiques of similar size), and deep expertise in insurance defense within Texas's $293.9 billion insurance market.

11. Conclusions & Recommendations

Key Findings

  1. Established market position: With 35 years in the Austin market and an AV Preeminent-rated managing partner, Skelton & Woody has deep credibility in business litigation and insurance defense.
  2. Broad practice differentiation: The combination of insurance defense + securities + construction + employment + appellate under one 17-attorney roof is uncommon among Austin boutiques. Only McGinnis Lochridge (70+ attorneys) matches this breadth, and at a much larger scale.
  3. Massive insurance market tailwind: Texas's $293.9B insurance premium base, $47B+ in 2024 insured losses, and doubling nuclear verdicts ($31.3B in 2024) all drive demand for the firm's core insurance defense practice.
  4. Growing metro: Austin's 2.55M-person MSA with 63,000+ business establishments, $99,897 median household income, and 2.33% annual population growth ensures a robust pipeline of commercial litigation.
  5. Competitive gap in rankings: The firm lacks Chambers USA recognition, which several smaller competitors have achieved. This represents both a challenge and an opportunity for brand positioning.

Recommendations for Further Research

  • TX SOS / Comptroller verification: Manually search SOSDirect and Comptroller franchise tax databases to confirm entity filing status, registered agent, and SOS file number.
  • Avvo / Martindale manual review: Visit both platforms in a browser to capture the full numerical rating, review counts, and endorsement details.
  • Google Business Profile: Check whether the firm has a claimed and active Google Business Profile with reviews.
  • PACER / court records: Search federal PACER database for Western District of Texas case filings involving Skelton & Woody to assess current case volume and types.
  • Billing rate discovery: Fee schedules and rate cards are not publicly available; direct engagement or legal billing database access (e.g., CounselLink) would be needed for firm-specific rates.
  • Succession planning assessment: With the co-founder's passing and the managing partner's 44-year career, understanding the next generation of leadership is critical for long-term positioning.
