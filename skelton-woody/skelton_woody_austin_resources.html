1. Immediate Actions (Free & High Impact)

These cost nothing or minimal investment and directly address visibility gaps identified in the research report. Each can be completed in under an hour.

Verify & Optimize Google Business Profile
Critical Priority · Free · ~45 minutes

The research report flagged the firm’s Google Business Profile status as unknown/unverified. Competitors like Burns Anderson, Thompson Coe, and McGinnis Lochridge all have optimized Google profiles with reviews, photos, and practice area details. A claimed GBP is essential for “litigation attorney Austin” and “insurance defense lawyer Austin” local search visibility.

  • Go to business.google.com and search for “Skelton & Woody PLLC”
  • If listed: claim and verify ownership (mail, phone, or video verification)
  • If not listed: create a new listing with address at 248 Addie Roy Rd, Ste B-302
  • Add all 13+ practice areas as service categories
  • Upload professional office and team photos
  • Encourage existing clients to leave reviews
Manage Google Business Profile →
Optimize Legal Directory Profiles
Critical Priority · Free · ~1 hour total

The firm already has listings on 8 legal directories (Avvo, Martindale-Hubbell, Super Lawyers, Justia, etc.). However, many may have basic or auto-generated profiles. Enriching these with updated practice area descriptions, notable results ($106M take-nothing verdict), and the AV Preeminent rating can dramatically improve search rankings.

  • Log in to each directory and claim full control of the profile
  • Update practice area descriptions with specific keywords (e.g., “insurance bad faith defense Austin”)
  • Add all attorney profiles, not just Hamp Skelton
  • Ensure consistent NAP (Name, Address, Phone) across all directories
Register on SAM.gov
High Priority · Free · ~30 minutes

SAM.gov registration makes the firm eligible to bid on federal government legal services contracts. The federal government contracts extensively for outside legal counsel, including litigation, insurance coverage opinions, and regulatory work—all within Skelton & Woody’s core competencies.

  • Visit sam.gov/entity-registration and create an account via Login.gov
  • Select “Full Registration” (needed for contract bidding)
  • Receive a free Unique Entity ID (UEI)
  • Processing takes up to 10 business days; renewal required annually
Register on SAM.gov →

2. Rankings & Directory Submissions

The research report identified a competitive gap in rankings: Skelton & Woody lacks Chambers USA recognition, which several smaller competitors have achieved. Addressing this is a high-impact strategic move.

Chambers USA Submission
Critical Priority · Free to submit · Multi-week process

Chambers and Partners ranks law firms and lawyers through independent researcher interviews with clients and peers. The firm should target the Litigation: General Commercial (Texas — Austin & Surrounds) category. Current ranked firms in this category include competitors identified in the competitive analysis.

  • Visit chambers.com and locate the submission portal
  • Research submissions typically open in spring; prepare client referee lists now
  • Focus on: representative matters, client references (6–10 strong referees), notable wins
  • Highlight: $106M take-nothing verdict, 1,000+ bad faith cases, Texas Supreme Court victories
  • Also consider: Insurance category submission given the firm’s deep insurance defense practice
View Chambers USA Rankings →
Best Lawyers in America & U.S. News Best Law Firms
High Priority · Free peer nomination · Annual cycle

Best Lawyers is a peer-reviewed ranking. U.S. News partners with Best Lawyers to produce the “Best Law Firms” rankings. Individual attorneys must first be recognized by Best Lawyers before the firm can be ranked.

  • Nominate firm attorneys at bestlawyers.com
  • Target categories: Commercial Litigation, Insurance Law, Construction Law, Appellate Practice
  • Encourage peer attorneys and clients to participate in the survey process
  • Once attorneys are recognized, submit for U.S. News Best Law Firms at bestlawfirms.usnews.com

3. Professional Certifications

Board certification through the Texas Board of Legal Specialization (TBLS) is a powerful differentiator. Only about 7,300 of Texas’s 90,000+ licensed attorneys hold board certification.

TBLS Insurance Law Certification
Critical Priority · Application fee varies · Exam required

Insurance Law is one of TBLS’s newest specialty areas, added in 2023. Because the certification is so new, very few Texas attorneys have earned it yet—making early certification a significant competitive differentiator. Given that insurance defense is one of the firm’s two primary practice pillars (1,000+ bad faith cases defended), having one or more attorneys earn this certification would be a major advantage.

  • Visit tbls.org for application requirements
  • Requirements include: substantial experience in the specialty, CLE hours, peer references, and a written exam
  • Applications are accepted online; see 2025 application cycle announcement
  • Additional relevant certifications: Civil Trial Law and Personal Injury Trial Law
TBLS Application Portal →
TBLS Civil Trial Law Certification
Medium Priority · Application fee varies · Exam required

Civil Trial Law is one of the most recognized TBLS certifications and directly aligns with the firm’s business litigation practice. Multiple attorneys could pursue this certification to strengthen the firm’s credentialed depth.

4. Bar Associations & Legal Organizations

Texas Association of Defense Counsel (TADC)
High Priority · $185–$295/year per attorney · Ongoing

TADC is the premier organization for Texas civil defense attorneys. Membership provides access to CLE, amicus brief participation, and networking with peers across the state. Essential for insurance defense practitioners.

  • Dues: $185/year (licensed ≤5 years) or $295/year (licensed >5 years)
  • 2026 Annual Meeting: September 23–27, 2026, San Luis Resort, Galveston, TX
  • CLE programming, amicus committee participation, practice-area sections
  • Networking with Texas defense bar; referral pipeline for multi-district cases
Join TADC →
Austin Bar Association
High Priority · $205–$280/yr per attorney · Annual

The Austin Bar (4,100+ members) is the central professional organization for the local legal community. 28 practice-area sections including Construction, Civil Litigation, Insurance Law, Civil Appellate, and ADR. Free Travis County Bench Book and 10–12 full-day CLE programs per year.

  • Dues: $250 (<5 yrs) / $280 (5+ yrs); solo/small firm: $205/$230
  • Annual Foundation Gala (January, Four Seasons Hotel) — high-visibility sponsorship
  • Bench Bar Conference provides direct interaction with Travis County judges
  • AYLA for associates under 40: $95/yr (newly licensed free)
Austin Bar Association →
Defense Research Institute (DRI)
High Priority · ~$395–$495/year (first year may be free via SLDO promo) · Annual

DRI is the largest international civil defense bar association (16,000+ members). TADC members may qualify for a free first-year DRI membership through periodic SLDO/NDO promotional campaigns—check dri.org/membership for current availability.

  • Insurance Law committee membership included with base membership
  • Free webinar CLE series (up to 8 hrs/yr)
  • National referral network—valuable for the firm’s local counsel services practice
  • Inquire about SLDO offer at membership@dri.org

8. Content Marketing & Digital Presence

The research report identified two key gaps: no blog/news section and minimal client testimonials (only one identified). These represent significant opportunities.

Launch Firm Blog / Insights Section
Critical Priority · Minimal cost (time investment) · Ongoing

A blog publishing 1–2 articles per month on insurance defense developments, Texas Supreme Court decisions, construction litigation updates, and legislative changes would dramatically improve SEO and position the firm as a thought leader. All Austin competitors of similar or smaller size maintain active content.

  • Topics: nuclear verdict trends, Texas insurance regulatory changes, bad faith defense strategies
  • Comply with Texas ethics rules on attorney advertising (Texas Disciplinary Rules of Professional Conduct, Rule 7)
  • Cross-post summaries on attorney LinkedIn profiles for organic reach
  • Consider submitting articles to Texas Lawyer, Texas Lawbook, and Insurance Journal
JD Supra Content Syndication
High Priority · Free · ~30 min per article

JD Supra is a free content syndication platform used by 50,000+ authors from 5,000+ firms. Publish articles, alerts, and blog posts that reach in-house counsel and claims professionals. LinkedIn Sync auto-shares content. Builds SEO backlinks and amplifies firm content beyond its own website.

Publish on JD Supra →
LinkedIn Thought Leadership Strategy
High Priority · Free · ~2 hours/week

Hamp Skelton already has a LinkedIn profile. 9 in 10 decision-makers say they are more receptive to outreach from companies that produce high-quality thought leadership (2024 Edelman-LinkedIn B2B Thought Leadership Impact Report). A structured program with 2–3 posts per week per attorney covering case law analysis, legislative updates, and conference takeaways would expand the firm’s digital footprint. Repurpose blog content as LinkedIn articles and JD Supra syndication.

SEO & Meta Tag Optimization
Medium Priority · Low cost · One-time + maintenance

The research report noted “basic” SEO/meta tags. Enhanced meta descriptions targeting “Austin insurance defense lawyer,” “Texas bad faith litigation,” and “Austin business litigation firm” would improve organic search rankings. Consider adding schema markup (LegalService, Attorney) for rich search results.
