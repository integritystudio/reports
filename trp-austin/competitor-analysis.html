TRP Austin - Competitive SWOT Analysis

Strengths

  • 75+ combined years of leadership experience (Skelton 45+ yrs, Kirkpatrick 30+ yrs)
  • Both principals hold MBAs and are licensed TX brokers
  • Rare dual residential + commercial + investment service offering in a boutique firm
  • Due diligence and business valuation as a service differentiator (few competitors offer this)
  • 1031 exchange expertise (only EREG matches this in boutique space)
  • Austin-San Antonio corridor coverage matches market growth patterns
  • Principal-led engagement; clients work directly with senior brokers

Weaknesses

  • Minimal online review presence (no Google rating visible, no Zillow reviews, limited Yelp)
  • Website issues (403 errors observed during research)
  • No press mentions, media coverage, or industry awards found
  • Small team limits capacity for large-scale assignments
  • P.O. Box address (vs. competitors with prominent office locations)
  • No visible social media or content marketing presence
  • Competitors like Devora Realty have achieved stronger digital footprints with similar service offerings

Opportunities

  • Build Google/Yelp review presence (request reviews from past clients)
  • Position due diligence + valuation + 1031 exchange as unique bundled offering
  • Content marketing on Austin investment trends, 1031 strategies, site selection insights
  • Target Westlake/78746 market where St. Croix and CLD Realty are the main boutique players
  • Austin buyer's market (2026) creates demand for investment advisory services
  • I-35 corridor growth (Austin-San Antonio) aligns with TRP's coverage area
  • Institutional clients may prefer boutique firms over large national players for personalized service

Threats

  • Major firms (CBRE, JLL, C&W) have dominant brand recognition and resources
  • Devora Realty, Sierra Commercial, and EREG offer nearly identical service mixes
  • Large residential brokerages (Compass, KW) dominate residential search and lead generation
  • Tech-forward firms attract younger buyers/sellers with digital tools
  • Austin market softening (median price down 4.1% YoY) may reduce transaction volume
  • Investment firms (Wildhorn, CCI, ACA) specialize in investor services at scale
  • Competitors with strong review profiles capture more organic search traffic

Key Competitive Insights

1. Closest Direct Competitors by Service Overlap

Three firms most closely match TRP's service mix of residential + commercial + investment + development:

  • Devora Realty - Residential, commercial, multifamily, development, and land. CCIM-designated broker. Perfect 5.0 Google rating with 45 reviews. Strongest overall competitor by service match and online presence.
  • Sierra Commercial - Commercial, select residential, site selection, development consulting, investment strategy, property management. Very similar positioning but focused on Hill Country. Minimal online review footprint.
  • Executive Real Estate Group (EREG) - Commercial brokerage, development, 1031 exchanges, site selection, leasing. Only other boutique firm in Austin explicitly offering 1031 exchange services alongside site selection. CEO-led model mirrors TRP's principal-led approach.

2. Westlake / 78746 Competitive Landscape

Two notable competitors operate directly in the Westlake/78746 area where TRP may target high-net-worth clients:

  • St. Croix Capital Realty Advisors (901 S Mopac, 78746) - Established 1982; commercial investment sales, asset management, debt/equity sourcing. The longest-operating boutique commercial firm in this submarket.
  • CLD Realty (1004 Mopac Circle, 78746) - Retail-focused commercial brokerage with development and land services. $6.5M revenue.
  • Moreland Properties dominates luxury residential in Westlake/Lake Austin with 75+ agents and 35+ year brand recognition.

3. Due Diligence / Business Valuation Gap in Market

TRP's offering of due diligence and business valuation services is a significant differentiator. Among all competitors researched, no other boutique Austin real estate brokerage explicitly markets combined due diligence and business valuation alongside brokerage services. Dedicated valuation firms (Benaglio Group, BizWorth, INNP Consulting) operate separately from brokerage. TRP could position this as its primary competitive advantage.

4. Online Presence Benchmark

Review counts and ratings across the competitor set highlight a wide range:

  • Top Tier: Spyglass Realty (1,800+ five-star reviews), Moreland Properties (66 Yelp reviews)
  • Mid Tier: Devora Realty (45 Google reviews, 5.0 stars), Asterra (10 reviews, 4.0 stars)
  • Low/No Presence: TRP Austin, Sierra Commercial, EREG, St. Croix, CLD Realty, Capital Commercial - minimal or no public reviews

The boutique commercial firms tend to have weaker online review profiles than residential-focused firms, suggesting this is an industry-wide pattern rather than unique to TRP. However, Devora Realty demonstrates that a boutique firm doing both residential and commercial can achieve strong review numbers.

5. Austin Market Context (Feb 2026)

  • Median home price: $540,000 (down 4.1% YoY) - shifted to buyer's market in late 2025
  • Mortgage rates: 6.65% (30-yr fixed), 5.77% (15-yr fixed)
  • Population growth continues to drive demand (metro Austin grew 33% 2010-2020)
  • Inventory increasing, giving buyers more options
  • Compass-Anywhere merger creating a 340,000-agent residential mega-brokerage
  • Investment opportunities remain strong in workforce multifamily and I-35 corridor industrial
