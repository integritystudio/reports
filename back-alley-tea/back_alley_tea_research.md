## Executive Summary

Back Alley Tea is an Austin-based pop-up tea service founded by **Nick Taylor** and operated from a residential address in the East Riverside-Oltorf neighborhood (78741). The concept blends specialty tea service with community building — a model aligned with the two fastest-growing forces in the U.S. tea market: (1) Gen Z and millennial consumers who treat tea as *social currency*, and (2) the rise of intentional, quality-first pop-up tea formats exemplified locally by Mori Matcha. The North America tea market is valued at **USD 44.05B in 2026** with a 4.85% CAGR; specialty segments grow at 6%+. Austin's tea landscape is fragmented between boba chains and a handful of loose-leaf houses — leaving meaningful whitespace for community-anchored, experiential tea programming. Public footprint is currently limited to Instagram; building baseline discovery assets is the highest-leverage next step.

---

## 1. Company Overview

**Back Alley Tea** is a pop-up tea service. Two intertwined functions:

- **Pop-up tea service** — preparing and serving tea outside of a fixed retail storefront.
- **Community building** — using tea service as a gathering format.

The pop-up format is rising in Austin's specialty beverage scene. [Mori Matcha's founder Tiffany Nguyen told Axios Austin](https://www.axios.com/local/austin/2026/03/05/austin-pop-up-fuels-matcha-craze) the pop-up model was deliberate: it "allowed the team to focus on quality and to build something organically without overextending too quickly."

## 11. Conclusions & Recommendations

### Key Findings

1. **Market timing is favorable** — $44.05B NA market, 6%+ specialty CAGR, validated Austin pop-up appetite (Mori Matcha).
2. **Concept differentiation is real but unarticulated publicly** — "pop-up tea + community" distinguishes from boba and matcha-only pop-ups, but no public messaging communicates this.
3. **Discovery infrastructure is missing** — no website / GBP / Yelp / directory presence. Highest-leverage gap.
4. **Neighborhood is well-matched** — young, dense, renter-dominant.
5. **Regulatory clarity needed** — prepared-beverage permitting beyond home kitchen.

### Recommended Next Steps (Research)

- Confirm permitting model with Austin Public Health
- Verify Texas SOS / Travis County DBA status
- Owner interview: cadence, ticket size, revenue mix, sourcing, 12-month goals
- Competitive deep-dive on Mori / Central / Magick
- Survey partner-venue ecosystem (yoga studios, bookshops, galleries, co-working)

### Recommended Operational Moves

- **Claim a Google Business Profile** (service-area mode)
- **One-page website** (concept, calendar, email capture, booking)
- **Booking platform** (Partiful / Eventbrite / Tock)
- **Newsletter** — community-building needs an owned audience channel
- **Publish menu & pricing** — discovery + conversion both lift
