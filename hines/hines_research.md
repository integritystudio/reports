## Executive Summary

Hines Interests Limited Partnership is one of the world's largest privately held real estate investment, development, and management firms, founded in Houston, Texas in 1957 by Gerald D. Hines. As of December 31, 2025, the company manages **$91.7 billion in AUM** across 30 countries, operates ~4,600 employees, and maintains an active portfolio spanning 837 properties and over 299 million square feet. Hines has a documented and active footprint in the Austin metro market — including the completed T3 Eastside mass-timber office project, a ground lease for a potential 1.9M SF redevelopment at 333 Guadalupe Street downtown, and a prior master-planned community (Mirador, sold to Starwood Capital Group in April 2025 for an estimated $800M in a portfolio transaction). The firm is a recognized ESG leader, having co-founded the LEED certification program in 1998, and has earned GRESB 5-star ratings across four flagship funds. Austin's office market experienced peak distress in early 2025 (~29% vacancy, Cushman & Wakefield) before recovering to 22.3% by Q4 2025 (Colliers), still elevated but declining as supply absorption improves. Durable MSA fundamentals (51% household growth 2014–2024, 2.3M metro population) support continued recovery through 2026–2027.

## Conclusions & Recommendations

### Key Findings

- **Market position:** Hines is a top-tier global private RE operator with $91.7B AUM, 69 years of history, and genuine ESG leadership. Competes across different service lines with Brookfield, CBRE, JLL, and Prologis.
- **Austin footprint:** Active and growing — T3 Eastside (complete), 333 Guadalupe (ground lease, up to 1.9M sq ft), Domain office project (608K sq ft pipeline). Mirador community divested April 2025 at favorable pricing.
- **Market timing:** Austin office vacancy peaked at ~29% (Q1 2025) and recovered to 22.3% (Q4 2025, Colliers) as net absorption turned positive (~1.1M sq ft citywide, Colliers — single-broker estimate). Downtown remains distressed (~32.4%, Colliers). Exceptional MSA fundamentals (1.7% annual growth, $93K median income, 51% household growth) support continued mid-cycle recovery through 2026–2027.
- **Cultural risk:** Glassdoor 2025 reviews flag material cultural shift — bonus cuts, reduced autonomy. Monitor as Laura Hines-Pierce assumes full leadership authority.
- **Sustainability leadership:** LEED co-founder + GRESB 5-star are genuine institutional differentiators, especially for European and Asian LP capital increasingly demanding ESG-aligned products.

### Recommendations

- Conduct direct outreach to Hines Austin project team for 333 Guadalupe timeline and Domain status
- Request Hines Global Income Trust investor materials (hinesglobalincometrust.com) for portfolio-level financials
- Monitor Austin office absorption quarterly (Cushman & Wakefield MarketBeats) to assess 333 Guadalupe development trigger
- Review 2024 Sustainability Report PDF for complete ESG metrics
- Track Glassdoor sentiment through 2026 to assess cultural stabilization under Laura Hines-Pierce
- Verify TX SOS entity registration via SOSDirect for legal due diligence
