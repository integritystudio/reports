### Lifetime Value (LTV)

**LTV Calculation Framework:**
```
LTV = (ARPU × Gross Margin) / Churn Rate

Where:
├── ARPU = Average Revenue Per User (monthly or annual)
├── Gross Margin = (Revenue - COGS) / Revenue
└── Churn Rate = Monthly customer churn % (or 1/lifetime months)
```

**Segment-Specific LTV Analysis:**

**1. Self-Serve Segment**
```
├── Average ACV: $8,400 ($700/month)
├── Gross margin: 85% (low touch, infrastructure costs only)
├── Monthly churn: 3.5%
├── Average customer lifetime: 28.6 months
└── LTV = ($700 × 0.85) / 0.035 = $17,000

Alternative (cohort-based):
└── LTV = $700 × 28.6 months × 0.85 = $17,017
```

**2. SMB Segment**
```
├── Average ACV: $28,000 ($2,333/month)
├── Gross margin: 87%
├── Monthly churn: 2.2%
├── Average customer lifetime: 45.5 months
└── LTV = ($2,333 × 0.87) / 0.022 = $92,227
```

**3. Mid-Market Segment**
```
├── Average ACV: $85,000 ($7,083/month)
├── Gross margin: 88%
├── Monthly churn: 1.5%
├── Average customer lifetime: 66.7 months
├── Expansion revenue: 15% annual upsell
└── LTV = ($7,083 × 0.88) / 0.015 × 1.15 = $475,858
```

**4. Enterprise Segment**
```
├── Average ACV: $220,000 ($18,333/month)
├── Gross margin: 90% (economies of scale)
├── Monthly churn: 0.8%
├── Average customer lifetime: 125 months (10.4 years)
├── Expansion revenue: 25% annual upsell
└── LTV = ($18,333 × 0.90) / 0.008 × 1.25 = $2,578,078
```

= $2,550 + $18,400 + $166,600 + $774,000

### Gross Margin Economics

**SaaS Infrastructure Cost Structure:**

```
Revenue: $100,000 MRR
├── Cloud infrastructure (AWS/GCP): $8,000 (8%)
│   ├── Data ingestion/storage: $4,500
│   ├── Compute (analysis pipelines): $2,200
│   └── Data egress: $1,300
├── Third-party APIs/tools: $2,000 (2%)
├── Customer success (allocated): $3,500 (3.5%)
└── COGS total: $13,500

Gross Margin = ($100K - $13.5K) / $100K = 86.5%
```

**Margin Targets by Stage:**
- **Seed**: 70-80% (acceptable, still optimizing infrastructure)
- **Series A**: 80-85% (improving efficiency, economies of scale)
- **Series B+**: 85-90% (mature cost structure, target 88%+)

**Margin Improvement Levers:**
1. **Infrastructure optimization**: Reserved instances, spot pricing
2. **Data tiering**: Hot vs. cold storage strategies
3. **Customer self-service**: Reduce CS overhead per customer
4. **Pricing model alignment**: Charge for high-cost features

### Key Metric Definitions for Investors

**Magic Number**
```
Magic Number = (Net New ARR in Quarter) / (Sales & Marketing Spend in Prior Quarter)

Example:
├── Q1 S&M spend: $400K
├── Q2 net new ARR: $500K
└── Magic Number = $500K / $400K = 1.25

Interpretation:
├── <0.5: Inefficient, premature scaling
├── 0.5-0.75: Acceptable, needs optimization
├── 0.75-1.0: Good, ready to scale
├── 1.0-1.5: Excellent, pour fuel on fire
└── >1.5: Exceptional, unicorn potential
```

**Burn Multiple**
```
Burn Multiple = Net Burn / Net New ARR

Example:
├── Quarterly net burn: $800K
├── Net new ARR added: $600K
└── Burn Multiple = $800K / $600K = 1.33

Interpretation:
├── <1.0: Exceptional capital efficiency
├── 1.0-1.5: Good, sustainable growth
├── 1.5-2.0: Acceptable for high-growth stage
└── >2.0: Poor efficiency, risk of down round
```

**Rule of 40**
```
Rule of 40 = Revenue Growth Rate + Profit Margin

Example:
├── YoY revenue growth: 180%
├── Operating margin: -35% (burning for growth)
└── Score = 180% - 35% = 145% (exceptional)

Benchmarks:
├── >40%: Meeting the rule (public company standard)
├── >60%: Strong performance (VC-backed growth)
├── >100%: Exceptional (hypergrowth, acceptable burn)
└── <20%: Warning (slow growth + unprofitable)
```

### Pricing Metrics (Unit Economics)

**Primary Pricing Dimension: Traces/Events**
- **Pros**: Aligns with value (more AI usage = more monitoring need)
- **Pros**: Automatic expansion as customer grows
- **Cons**: Can create sticker shock for high-volume use cases
- **Mitigation**: Include generous base allowances, volume discounts

**Secondary Dimension: Data Retention**
- **Rationale**: Long-term retention has real infrastructure costs
- **Pricing**: Exponential tiers (7 days free, 30 days included, 90 days +$X, 1 year +$Y)
- **Expansion**: Compliance requirements often drive upgrades

**Tertiary Dimension: Advanced Features**
- **AI-powered root cause analysis**: $400-$600/month add-on
- **Anomaly detection**: Included in Pro+, separate in Team
- **Custom integrations**: $2K-$10K one-time + $200/mo maintenance
- **Multi-tenancy/White-labeling**: Enterprise only, +30-50% premium

### Revenue Model Trade-Offs

**When to Choose Usage-Based:**

**Best For:**
- High variance in customer usage patterns
- Naturally growing consumption (AI workloads scale with business)
- Developer-first/PLG motion (low friction to start)
- Competitive markets (reduce commitment barriers)

**AI Observability Fit**: Excellent
- Trace volume grows with model deployments
- Aligns cost with customer value realization
- Enables "land and expand" without sales intervention

**Risks to Mitigate:**
- Revenue unpredictability (monthly forecasting challenges)
- Customer "bill shock" (set up alerts, recommend annual commits)
- Sales comp complexity (tie to ACV, not just new logos)

---

**When to Choose Subscription:**

**Best For:**
- Predictable capacity needs (fixed seats, fixed infrastructure)
- High-touch enterprise sales (expect commitment)
- Mature markets with established budgets
- Investors demand predictable revenue (late-stage)

**AI Observability Fit**: Moderate
- Works for enterprise tier (multi-year deals, predictable base)
- Challenges: Doesn't capture organic growth automatically
- Best as hybrid: Base subscription + usage overage

---

**Hybrid Model (Recommended):**

**Structure:**
```
Monthly Bill Composition:
├── Base subscription: $1,500 (50M traces, 90-day retention)
├── Overage charges: $800 (20M additional traces @ $0.04/1K)
├── Add-ons: $400 (cost optimization module)
└── Total: $2,700 (33% usage-based, 67% subscription)

Benefits:
├── Predictable base (helps with revenue forecasting)
├── Automatic expansion (captures growth without sales friction)
├── Customer safety net (base allowance prevents overage anxiety)
└── Margin optimization (base covers fixed costs, overage is high-margin)
```

**Best Practices:**
- Set base allowance at 60-70th percentile of usage (most customers fit)
- Overage pricing at slight premium (encourage right-sizing annually)
- Annual re-negotiation to reset baselines (capture sustained growth)
