## Executive Summary

The home health care industry faces unprecedented challenges with a 79.2% turnover rate in 2024, making strategic hiring critical for success. This research identifies key characteristics, screening strategies, and best practices for identifying exceptional candidates for Leora Home Health, a nurse-owned and operated company in Austin.

---

## 1. Employee Performance Characteristics & Indicators

### Top Performer Traits in Home Health Care

**Essential Soft Skills (Ranked by Importance):**
1. **Empathy** - Most critical trait, helps deliver better care and prevents burnout
2. **Patience** - Essential for challenging situations, especially with dementia patients
3. **Communication** - Critical for understanding patient needs and relaying information
4. **Compassion** - Demonstrated through daily task assistance
5. **Adaptability** - Ability to handle changing situations quickly
6. **Self-motivation** - Crucial for independent work environments

**Technical Competencies:**
- Medical equipment proficiency (oxygen machines, blood pressure monitors)
- Medication management and wound care capabilities
- Basic computer skills for applications and documentation
- Physical strength and stamina for patient mobility assistance
- Time management for multiple patient schedules

**Professional Attributes:**
- Maintains professional appearance and confidentiality
- High attention to detail and accuracy
- Demonstrates tact in sensitive situations
- Balances independence with team collaboration

4. **Organizational support** - When caregivers feel valued
5. **Clear processes** - Disorganization is a primary turnover driver

- Minnesota 2024 minimum: $19/hour (up to $22.50 for experienced)
- Median annual wage: $34,900 (May 2024)

## 4. Red Flags & Warning Signs

### Critical Screening Red Flags

**Background Check Concerns:**
- Violent offenses
- Healthcare fraud history
- Drug-related misdemeanors
- Patient abuse allegations
- Theft convictions
- Lying about employment history

**Behavioral Red Flags:**
- Discomfort with monitored environments
- Poor attitude or communication during interviews
- Inability to provide specific care examples
- Lack of empathy in responses
- Resistance to background checks
- Pushy or rushed decision-making

**Professional Warning Signs:**
- Frequent job changes without explanation
- Consistently late or unreliable in past roles
- Unable to explain gaps in employment
- Poor references from healthcare settings
- Lack of interest in patient outcomes

## 5. Best Practices for Identifying High-Potential Candidates

### Screening Strategies

**Multi-Stage Assessment Process:**
1. **Initial Screening:** Resume review for required certifications
2. **Behavioral Assessment:** Personality and cognitive testing
3. **Structured Interview:** Using STAR method questions
4. **Practical Demonstration:** Care scenario simulations
5. **Reference Verification:** Focus on healthcare-specific references
6. **Background Check:** Comprehensive criminal and credential verification

### Predictive Assessment Tools
- **Cognitive tests:** Problem-solving and learning agility (strongest performance predictor)
- **Personality assessments:** Big Five traits alignment
- **Job simulations:** Virtual care scenarios
- **Behavioral interviews:** Past performance as future indicator

**Assessment Validity:**
- Traditional interviews: 0.2 validity coefficient
- Structured assessments: 0.5+ validity coefficient
- ROI potential: 3.2x within first year

## 8. Practical Screening Questions & Interview Strategies

### Behavioral Interview Questions (STAR Method)

**Empathy & Compassion:**
- "Tell me about a time you went above and beyond for a patient"
- "Describe a situation where you had to comfort a distressed family member"

**Adaptability & Problem-Solving:**
- "Share an example of handling an unexpected medical emergency at home"
- "How did you manage when a patient refused necessary care?"

**Communication & Teamwork:**
- "Describe collaborating with other healthcare providers on a complex case"
- "Tell me about effectively communicating bad news to a patient/family"

**Reliability & Work Ethic:**
- "Share an example of maintaining patient care during personal challenges"
- "Describe managing multiple patients with competing priorities"

### Situational Assessment Scenarios

**Scenario 1: Dementia Patient**
"An elderly patient with dementia becomes agitated and refuses medication. How do you respond?"

**Scenario 2: Family Conflict**
"Family members disagree about care approach. How do you navigate this?"

**Scenario 3: Safety Concern**
"You notice unsafe conditions in a patient's home. What steps do you take?"
