## Executive Summary

Integrity Studio is an Austin-based AI observability and compliance platform founded in 2024. The company offers LLM monitoring, distributed tracing, EU AI Act compliance tooling, and governance solutions for production AI environments. With pricing from free to $99/month (and custom enterprise tiers), it targets a rapidly growing market segment: the global LLM observability platform market was valued at $510.5M in 2024 and is projected to reach $8.08B by 2034 at a 31.8% CAGR. The company operates from Austin, TX, with a team of 2–10 employees including leadership across engineering, data science, policy, and compliance. Integrity Studio competes against well-funded platforms like Datadog LLM, Arize, LangSmith, Langfuse, and Helicone, differentiating through its combined observability + EU AI Act compliance positioning and nonprofit-sector consultancy arm. The Austin tech ecosystem—home to 203,000+ tech workers and 7,400 AI specialists—provides a strong talent base and customer pipeline.

### Positioning Assessment

**Strengths:**
- Unique dual positioning: observability + EU AI Act compliance tooling
- Nonprofit consultancy addresses underserved market (89% using AI, 4% have training budgets)
- OpenTelemetry-native architecture aligns with standardization trend
- Competitive pricing ($99/mo Pro) undercuts enterprise incumbents
- Well-rounded leadership (engineering, data science, policy, legal)

**Challenges:**
- Early-stage (2024) vs. well-funded incumbents (Datadog: $2.1B revenue)
- No independent reviews on G2, Capterra, or Clutch
- Small team (2–10) limits enterprise support capacity
- Open-source competitors offer $0 self-hosted options

**Opportunities:**
- EU AI Act full applicability (Aug 2026) creates compliance urgency
- Nonprofit AI consultancy targets segment with 60% lacking expertise
- LLM observability market growing at 31.8% CAGR
- Agentic AI monitoring is emerging, under-served category

**Threats:**
- APM incumbents (Datadog, New Relic) aggressively adding LLM monitoring
- Langfuse (open source, MIT) growing rapidly
- Cloud providers could bundle observability features
- Price pressure from free-tier competitors
